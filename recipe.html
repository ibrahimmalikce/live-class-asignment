My recipe for jollof rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a vibrant, flavorful West African dish made with rice simmered in a rich blend of tomatoes, peppers, onions, and aromatic spices. Known for its bright red color and smoky, savory taste, it’s a party favorite often served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or salad. This one-pot wonder is not only delicious but also a symbol of celebration and togetherness in Nigerian homes.

Recipe information

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Servings: 4-5 servings
  • Difficulty: Moderate

Recipe ingredients

  • 3 cups of parboiled basmati rice
  • 5 medium-sized tomoatoes
  • 3 red bell (Tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnet (ata rodo), adjust to taste
  • 1 large onion (half for blending, half for frying)
  • 3 tablespoon of tomato paste (prefferably gino brand)
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoon of thyme
  • 2 teaspoon of curry powder
  • Seasoning cube (to taste)
  • 2 bay leaves (optional,but adds nice aroma)
  • salt to taste

Instructions

  • Blend the base

    Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and half of the onion into a smooth paste.

  • Parboil rice

    Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Parboil for about 5 minutes, then drain and set aside.

  • Cook the sauce

    Heat oil in a pot, add chopped onions, and fry until translucent. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 3–5 minutes to reduce sourness. Add blended pepper mix and cook on medium heat for 15–20 minutes, stirring often until oil separates from the sauce.

  • Season the sauce

    Add thyme, curry powder, seasoning cubes, bay leaves, and salt. Stir well.

  • Add stock and rice

    Pour in chicken stock, bring to a boil, then add the parboiled rice. Stir so the rice is evenly coated with the sauce.

  • Steam, don’t stir too much

    Cover the pot tightly (you can use foil to trap steam). Reduce heat to low and let it cook for 20–30 minutes until rice is tender.

  • Final touches

    Stir gently from bottom to top to mix. If needed, sprinkle a little water, cover, and steam for 5 more minutes.

  • Serve

    Serve hot with fried plantain, grilled chicken, or salad.

Tip

For that authentic “party jollof” smoky flavor, let the rice cook on low heat and allow it to slightly toast at the bottom of the pot just enough to give a smoky aroma without burning too much.
